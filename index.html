Who Will Tax Reform Affect?

When it comes to tax reform, the results will be a mixed outcome for American citizens. Both the Senate and the House bill are similar because both double the standard deduction for 2018, increase the child tax credit, and make tax contingencies permanent for corporations. There are slight differences between the two: The House bill would make the provisions ineradicable for individuals and big businesses while the Senate bill makes most of the provisions permanent for businesses but provisions for single taxpayers will sunset at the end of 2025. The biggest winners from the bills are small business owners and corporations who will see the corporate tax rate go down from 35 percent to 20 percent.

WINNER: Small businesses and big corporations. This graph shows how corporate profits are still profitable after tax.

LOSER: People with student loans could get hurt. Presently, individuals can itemize certain payments. Under the Senate and House versions, most of these deductions will be stripped away including deductions for moving charges and student loan interests.

LOSER: People with alimony payments since the House GOP plan eliminates the deduction.
LOSER: Wealthy private colleges and universities. They will get a 1.4 percent excise tax on their endowments.
